TAROKO GORGE/ɡɔːdʒ/
- Taroko Gorge comprises the world's deepest/di:'pest/ 'marble 'canyon, as well as many stunning rugged/ˈrʌɡ.ɪd/ cliffs and subtropical forests.
- It formed over millions of years by the jade-coloured Liwu River, the gorge possesses/pəˈzeses/ no fewer than 27 peaks/piːks/.
- Taroko was established/ɪˈstæb.lɪʃt/ as a National Park in 1986 and entry is free.

SHIFEN/ʃi:fơn/ WATERFALL
- Shifen Waterfall is a scenic/ˈsiː.nɪk/ waterfall located on the upper reaches of the Keelung/ki:lʌn/ River in Pingxi District, New Tai'pei City, Taiwan.
- The falls have a total height/hait/ of 20 meters (66 feet) and a width of 40 meters (130 feet), making it Taiwan's widest/waidest/ waterfall.
- It is a cascade/kæsˈkeɪd/ waterfall in which the water flows/fləʊs/ in one direction and the rock is sloped/sləʊpt/ in the opposite/ˈɒp.ə.zɪt/.
	
SUN MOON LAKE
- Sun Moon Lake is a lake in 'Yuchi Township, 'Nantou County, Taiwan.
- It is the largest body of water in Taiwan.
- Sun Moon Lake surrounds/səˈraʊnd/ a tiny/ˈtaɪ.ni/ island/ˈaɪ.lənd/ called Lalu.
- The east side of the lake resembles/rɪˈzem.bəls/ the sun while the west side resembles the moon, hence the name.
- Sun Moon Lake is located 748m (2,454 ft) above the sea level.
